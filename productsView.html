Acids

Acids are chemical compounds that have a sour taste and turn litmus paper red. They can have a wide range of uses, including in food and beverage production, cleaning products, and industrial processes.

  • High reactivity
  • Sour taste
  • Turn litmus paper red

Uses:

  • Food and beverage production
  • Cleaning products
  • Industrial processes

Bases

Bases are chemical compounds that have a bitter taste and turn litmus paper blue. They can be used in a variety of applications, such as in cleaning products, personal care products, and industrial processes.

  • Alkaline nature
  • Bitter taste
  • Turn litmus paper blue

Uses:

  • Cleaning products
  • Personal care products
  • Industrial processes

Solvents

Solvents are substances that are used to dissolve other substances to form a solution. They can be polar or non-polar, and have a wide range of uses, such as in paint and ink production, cleaning products, and chemical reactions.

  • Polar or non-polar
  • Ability to dissolve other substances

Uses:

  • Paint and ink production
  • Cleaning products
  • Chemical reactions

Catalysts

Catalysts are substances that increase the rate of a chemical reaction without being consumed. They can be used in a wide range of industrial processes, such as in the production of fertilizers, pharmaceuticals, and petrochemicals.

  • Increase reaction rate
  • Do not get consumed in reaction

Uses:

  • Production of fertilizers
  • Pharmaceuticals
  • Petrochemicals
